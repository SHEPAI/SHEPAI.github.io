The rocket concept


All civilizations become either spacefaring or extinct - Carl Sagen

The HP-1 rocket uses hydrogen gas to take it up 120,000 ft into the stratosphere. This has been done before, hydrogen balloons have problems such as:
-Lack of control
-loose gas when balloon pops
-Hydrogen is dangerous

These problems are addresed by the HP-1 rocket. The controlling of the balloon is done by a quadcopter, this means it is easy to take off and land in the same place using GPS and radio signals. The balloon uses a strong material, such as a light metal to prevent it from popping. The only remaining problem is that hydrogen is dangerous. There are no human pilots currently, and hydrogen is the best element for travel.
Once the stratosphere rocket has successfully worked, we will work on a stage 2 rocket to launch using normal rocket fuel. It costs approximatly 95p per gallon of rocket fuel (on mass production), we save nearly half the cost of launching a rocket as it has less distance to travel.
